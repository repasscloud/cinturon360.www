## Built for real travel operations

Cinturon360 is being designed as the travel governance, control, and visibility platform for TMCs and their clients.

That means performance is not just about page speed. It is about keeping travel operations moving. TMC teams need to service clients efficiently. Approvers need quick context. Finance and procurement teams need timely visibility. Leadership needs reporting that is clear, usable, and dependable.

The platform is being shaped around those operational realities from the start.

## What performance means in Cinturon360

For Cinturon360, performance means helping users move from issue to action with less friction.

### Responsive decision-making

Travel operations often depend on fast answers and clear next steps. Cinturon360 is being designed to support responsive workflows for:

- approvals
- policy checks
- exception handling
- booking visibility
- spend and compliance reporting
- downstream reporting and finance handoff

### Practical speed for TMCs and clients

The goal is to support both the TMC operating model and the client experience.

For TMCs, that means a platform that helps teams move quickly across bookings, approvals, policies, exceptions, and reporting demands.

For clients, that means a clear and reliable way to understand travel activity, policy position, and financial visibility without relying on fragmented tools or manual workarounds.

### Performance across the ecosystem

Cinturon360 is not intended to sit in isolation. It is being designed to work across the broader travel ecosystem, including TMC workflows, GDS and NDC content, direct supplier flows, approval paths, and downstream reporting or finance systems.

Performance therefore has to be considered across how data is received, processed, surfaced, and handed off.

## Why performance matters

A travel platform only adds value when it supports the pace of real operations.

If approvals are slow, they become a bottleneck.  
If policy visibility is unclear, exceptions increase.  
If reporting is delayed or difficult to use, teams fall back to manual spreadsheets and disconnected processes.

Cinturon360 is being shaped to reduce that friction and help TMCs and organisations operate with more speed, clarity, and control.

## Performance priorities

### 1. Fast day-to-day workflows

Users should be able to move through common tasks efficiently, especially where approvals, policy decisions, and operational updates are involved.

### 2. Clear and usable interfaces

The experience should stay focused, readable, and practical for teams that need to act quickly.

### 3. Reliable visibility at scale

As booking activity, entities, and reporting needs grow, the platform should continue to support dependable access to the information that matters.

### 4. Integration-aware architecture

Performance should hold up not only within the platform itself, but across the wider booking, approval, supplier, and reporting ecosystem around it.

## Built to support growth

Cinturon360 is being planned for TMCs and organisations that may need to support:

- multiple business entities
- different client structures
- policy variations by team or traveller
- different booking channels and suppliers
- growing reporting and compliance expectations

That makes performance a core part of platform trust.

## The standard Cinturon360 is aiming for

Cinturon360 is being built to help TMCs and their clients operate with speed, control, and visibility across the travel lifecycle.

Performance is part of that goal. Not as a marketing metric, but as a practical requirement for a platform intended to become the operating layer for modern travel management.
