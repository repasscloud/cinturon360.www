## Built for controlled travel operations

Cinturon360 is designed to help TMCs and their clients manage travel approvals and policy control in a structured, scalable way.

For many organisations, travel policy is not just about whether a booking is in or out of policy. It is also about who can approve spend, what thresholds apply, how exceptions are handled, and how decisions are tracked across the booking lifecycle.

Cinturon360 is intended to give TMCs, corporate clients, government organisations, and other travel programs a single platform for handling those controls more consistently.

## Why policy and approvals matter

Travel programs often involve multiple teams, travellers, budgets, approvers, suppliers, and booking channels.

Without a clear approval and policy framework, organisations can end up with:

- inconsistent approval paths
- unclear decision ownership
- too many manual workarounds
- weak visibility over exceptions
- limited auditability
- poor alignment between travel, finance, and procurement expectations

Cinturon360 is being positioned to reduce that operational friction by giving organisations a practical control layer around the booking ecosystem they already use.

## What Cinturon360 is designed to support

### Pre-trip approval workflows

Organisations often need bookings reviewed before they are confirmed.

Cinturon360 is intended to support approval workflows that can reflect the reality of how organisations operate, including different structures for:

- teams
- business units
- travellers
- managers
- finance approvers
- executive approvals
- special travel categories

The goal is to help TMCs and client organisations direct bookings to the right people at the right time, with the right context.

### Policy-based decisioning

Policy control needs to go beyond a simple yes-or-no rule.

Cinturon360 is designed to support policy logic based on a mix of operational and financial factors, such as:

- traveller type
- booking type
- cost centre
- route or destination
- supplier
- fare caps
- class of travel
- timing of booking
- exception conditions

This helps TMCs and client organisations apply structured travel governance without forcing every booking into a one-size-fits-all rule set.

### Out-of-policy handling

Not every travel booking will fit the standard path.

Cinturon360 is intended to support out-of-policy handling where organisations need to:

- identify that a booking is outside policy
- capture the reason
- escalate for review
- approve, reject, or redirect the request
- maintain a clear record of the final outcome

That is especially important for enterprise, government, and more controlled travel environments where auditability and decision traceability matter.

### Audit trail and accountability

A travel platform should not just process decisions. It should also make those decisions understandable later.

Cinturon360 is being designed to keep a usable record of:

- who initiated the request
- which rules were triggered
- who reviewed it
- when decisions were made
- what the final outcome was
- what exceptions or overrides were applied

That matters for operational follow-up, client servicing, reporting, governance, and internal review.

## Designed for TMCs and their clients

Cinturon360 is not being positioned only as an internal tool for one organisation.

It is being shaped as a platform that TMCs can use to support multiple client travel programs, each with their own:

- approval structures
- travel policies
- reporting expectations
- operational rules
- compliance requirements

That allows TMCs to deliver stronger governance and visibility to their clients without relying on fragmented manual processes.

## Where this is especially valuable

### Corporate travel programs

For corporate clients, policy and approvals help ensure bookings align with budget controls, internal hierarchy, and travel program expectations.

### Government and controlled environments

For government and more structured travel environments, approval pathways and decision records are often essential. Clear auditability and policy enforcement are part of day-to-day operational trust.

### Enterprise and multi-entity organisations

Larger organisations often need approvals and policy logic that reflect multiple business units, varied traveller roles, and different financial structures. Cinturon360 is being positioned to support that level of complexity.

### Smaller organisations working through a TMC

Smaller businesses may not need the same level of complexity, but they still benefit from a platform that helps ensure travel decisions are visible, controlled, and easy to follow.

## The role of Cinturon360

Cinturon360 is intended to become the platform where TMCs and their clients manage travel control across policy, approvals, exceptions, and accountability.

Rather than treating approval as a disconnected manual step, the platform is being shaped to make policy and approval part of the operating flow of modern travel management.

## What this helps organisations achieve

With the right structure in place, policy and approval workflows can help organisations:

- improve travel governance
- reduce manual review effort
- manage exceptions more consistently
- create clearer accountability
- support finance and procurement visibility
- strengthen audit readiness
- give TMCs a more structured service model for client delivery

Cinturon360 is being built to support that outcome through a more connected, practical, and scalable approach to travel policy and approvals.
